I'm Siarhei Baradulia, an Amsterdam-based Quality Engineer at JetBrains. I design systematic approaches to testing and build automation frameworks—increasingly for AI agents and the systems around them—that help teams see what works, what doesn't, and what needs attention before release.

My motto: "Shipping fast is good. Shipping good software fast is better."

I've spent more than 11 years in quality engineering, working on developer tools as well as web, desktop, and mobile products. Over time, my focus shifted to building systems that help teams spot release risks earlier. To me, that means reliable test results, clear criteria, automation that cuts noise instead of adding it, fast feedback, and tools developers can use directly.

My work at JetBrains has taken me from testing a plugin for technical writers and Compose Multiplatform to testing the platform for running and managing AI agents and MCP in software development workflows. I also build QA infrastructure for agents that analyze pull requests and turn their findings into QA artifacts people can review, with the whole workflow running in CI.

In my own projects, I evaluate AI systems using benchmarks to assess their results.

I'm also the creator of [SpeQA](https://plugins.jetbrains.com/plugin/31268-speqa--test-management-system), an IDE-native test management system for working with test cases and other QA artifacts directly in the IDE, with versioning and collaboration through Git.

On this blog, I write about quality engineering, AI-assisted development, AI agent workflows, and developer tools.

Outside work, I explore Amsterdam by bike—the best way I've found to get to know the city.
